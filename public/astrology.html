A Brief Introduction

What is Jyotish?

Jyotish — often translated as Vedic astrology — is one of the six Vedangas, the auxiliary sciences that support the study of the Vedas. Its literal meaning, "the science of light," points to what it actually does: reading the positions of celestial bodies as a way of understanding time, cycles and the circumstances a person is born into.

The Building Blocks

Five ideas everything else is built from

Before a chart can be read, a few core concepts need to be in place. These are the first things taught in Jyotish Praveena.

ग्रह

Graha (Planets)

The nine classical bodies — Sun, Moon, Mars, Mercury, Jupiter, Venus, Saturn, and the lunar nodes Rahu and Ketu — each carrying a distinct character and influence.

राशि

Rashi (Zodiac Signs)

The twelve 30-degree divisions of the zodiac belt, each with its own element, ruling planet and temperament — the backdrop against which the planets are placed.

भाव

Bhava (Houses)

Twelve houses radiating from the ascendant, each governing a specific area of life — self, wealth, communication, home, and so on through to loss and liberation.

नक्षत्र

Nakshatra (Lunar Mansions)

27 finer divisions the Moon travels through, each adding character detail a rashi alone can't capture — closer to a fingerprint than a broad category.

दशा

Dasha (Planetary Periods)

A timeline of which planet's influence is dominant at any given point in a person's life — the mechanism Jyotish uses to say not just what, but when.

Reading a Kundli

The chart is a map, not a verdict.

A kundli (birth chart) is drawn from the exact date, time and place of birth — it plots where each planet sat against the zodiac and houses at that moment. What follows is interpretation: an astrologer reads the relationships between planets, signs and houses to describe tendencies, timing and likely themes, not fixed outcomes.

This is why serious study matters. The same placement means something different depending on what else is happening in the chart — which is exactly what a structured course, rather than a weekend workshop, is built to teach properly.
